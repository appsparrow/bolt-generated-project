13 Card Rummy Rules

The goal of 13 Card Rummy is to arrange all 13 cards in your hand into valid sets and sequences. A valid declaration must meet the following requirements:

  • At least two sequences are mandatory.
  • One of the sequences must be a pure sequence (a sequence without jokers).
  • The remaining cards can be arranged into valid sets or sequences (pure or impure).

Key Terms

  • Sequence: A group of three or more consecutive cards of the same suit.
  • Pure Sequence: A sequence without any joker (e.g., 4♠, 5♠, 6♠).
  • Impure Sequence: A sequence that includes a joker (e.g., 7♦, 8♦, Joker).
  • Set: A group of three or four cards of the same rank but different suits (e.g., 9♠, 9♦, 9♣).
  • Joker: A card that can substitute for any other card to form sets or sequences.

Setup

  • Players: 2-6 players.
  • Deck: Use one or two standard 52-card decks with jokers, depending on the number of players.
  • Dealing:
    • Each player is dealt 13 cards.
    • The remaining cards form the stock pile (face down).
    • The top card of the stock pile is placed face up to start the discard pile.
    • A random card is selected as the wild joker.

Sequence Requirements

To make a valid declaration:

  • Two Sequences Are Mandatory:
    • At least one sequence must be a pure sequence.
    • The second sequence can be either pure or impure.
  • Pure Sequence: A sequence of three or more consecutive cards of the same suit without using a joker.
  • Impure Sequence: A sequence of three or more consecutive cards of the same suit that includes a joker.

Gameplay

Each turn consists of the following steps:

  • Draw: Pick one card from the stock pile or the top card from the discard pile.
  • Meld: Arrange your cards into valid sets and sequences.
  • Discard: End your turn by discarding one card to the discard pile.

Scoring

  • Points for Cards:
    • Number cards (2-10): Face value.
    • Face cards (J, Q, K): 10 points each.
    • Aces: 10 points.
    • Jokers: 0 points.
  • Penalty Points: Players with unarranged cards at the end of the game score penalty points equal to the total value of those cards. Maximum penalty: 80 points.
